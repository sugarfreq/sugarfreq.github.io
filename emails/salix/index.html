Let's Talk Bowel Preps

A conversation about bowel preps with Howard Franklin, MD, vice president of medical affairs and strategy at Salix Pharmaceuticals.

A:

According to patients' experiences and reported barriers to colonoscopy, most patients perceived the bowel preparation to be the most burdensome part of colonoscopy. More specifically, complaints regarding bowel preparation typically relate to the large volumes of liquids necessary to consume and the unpleasant taste. In fact, patient perceived negative experiences with bowel preparation consumption is an independent predictor of both a poor quality bowel preparation and a low rate of polyp detection. A low volume and pleasant tasting bowel prep that demonstrates comparable safety and efficacy to the currently available preps would fulfill a clinical need.
 

A:

There is a direct correlation between ADR and bowel prep quality; ADR decreases as the quality of bowel prep decreases, with a steep decline seen as the quality of the preparation is decreased from fair to poor. Secondarily, if the prep is of sub-optimal quality, the recommendation is for more frequent follow up colonoscopy, which adds costs and burden to the patient and the health care system. In addition, right-sided advanced colon cancer shows a significantly worse prognosis than left-sided colon cancer, so it is important to have excellent cleansing of the right colon prior to colonoscopy.

Q:

Why is achieving a high-quality bowel prep so important for improving adenoma detection rate (ADR)?

Answer

A:

There is a direct correlation between ADR and bowel prep quality; ADR decreases as the quality of bowel prep decreases, with a steep decline seen as the quality of the preparation is decreased from fair to poor. Secondarily, if the prep is of sub-optimal quality, the recommendation is for more frequent follow up colonoscopy, which adds costs and burden to the patient and the health care system. In addition, right-sided advanced colon cancer shows a significantly worse prognosis than left-sided colon cancer, so it is important to have excellent cleansing of the right colon prior to colonoscopy.

INDICATIONS
PLENVU (polyethylene glycol 3350, sodium ascorbate, sodium sulfate, ascorbic acid, sodium chloride and potassium chloride for oral solution) is an osmotic laxative indicated for cleansing of the colon in preparation for colonoscopy in adults.

IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION
PLENVU is contraindicated in patients with gastrointestinal (GI) obstruction, bowel perforation, gastric retention, ileus, toxic megacolon, and hypersensitivity to any of its ingredients.
